•     Filming.
Filming 360-video can become expensive, especially when it comes to stitching the footage seamlessly from the array of cameras used to capture all-around a vantage point. It requires specialist knowledge to avoid the many fatal mistakes like back seam placement or incorrect camera configurations that can ruin an entire shoot. A good way to think about a 360 shoot is that every shot is basically a VFX place that will require compositing work to complete, so think about how to frame to have the most material possible to help that composite, whether it's overlap, or recording clean shots for removing equipment & crew or taking careful notes to reassemble shots later in post-production.

Real-time 6DOF applications, on the other hand, require a skill set like those of game developers. This is a multidisciplinary job description that touches on the knowledge of design, programming, interactivity, UI, and real-time rendering. VR adds another complexity on top of that, as the material created needs to meet a higher frame rate performance to avoid user nausea.
