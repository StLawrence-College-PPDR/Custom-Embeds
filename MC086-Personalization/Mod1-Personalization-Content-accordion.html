•     Image Personalization.
In the same way that your headline message should feel relevant to each digital customer walking through your virtual doors, the imagery they see should reflect a solution to their needs as well. For example, a fitness store might change the imagery from someone doing yoga in a desert to someone skiing based on the geographic location of their customer. Often, the headline or anchoring image on your website is called the ‘hero image’ – it represents the solution to the problem users are working to solve.

•     Blog Post Personalization.
If you use a lot of blog and content marketing (which you should), then ensuring that suggested articles are relevant to the needs of each user matters. If you have content that talks about how to vet a service you offer, or how to select the right version of a product that you sell for someone’s needs, then be sure to put it in front of the customer who is trying to accomplish those goals.

•     Product or Feature Personalization
You have a variety of services or products you sell. Knowing which one each customer is seeking out matters – this should be what is highlighted to your customer when they arrive on your website. You may even emphasize different sales offerings to different customers based on which product, service, or service feature they are interested in.
