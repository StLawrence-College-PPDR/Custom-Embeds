•     Call to Action Personalization.
You’ve heard about the importance of a Call to Action on your website. Calls to Action nudge visitors to take a specific action – like sign up for a newsletter, request more information on a service, or buy a product. But can one Call to Action fit all of your customers? Probably not – they want slightly different things from the same business. Personalizing the Call to Action gives customers the pathway to the action that matters to them and cuts out what otherwise might feel like noise to them (even if it’s exactly what a different customer would want).

•     Navigation/Menu Personalization.
Personalizing a menu or navigation bar isn’t necessarily about changing it for every customer, so much as re-ordering it so that the most direct pathway for each customers’ goal is presented first.
